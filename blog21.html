World Mental Health day

World Mental Health day is celebrated annually on October 10th globally. It is celebrated to create awareness and education on mental health to public all over the world. It was first celebrated in 1992, initiated by World Federation for Mental Health (WFMH)..

Why is world Mental Health Day important

Mental health is still continue to be highly stigmatized, in general public of India. As physical illness such as obesity, diabetes, arthritis etc. is treated, Mental illness like anxiety, depression, PTSD, OCD, ADHD should also be treated equally. To break the stigma, Mental Health day is the most needed. It is important because it provides us the forum to communicate with the public and to the patients suffering mental illness. As mental health is key to general health and a key to quality of life, everyone should realize the importance of Mental Health day. Globally the mental disorders account for approximately 25% of the disability that affects humanity. There are many treatments available to treat all sorts of mental disorders. The challenge is to have the access and availability to these treatments. The Mental Health day allows to provide advocacy and provide accurate information around good treatments that are available. Apart from their own surroundings, they contribute to the larger society. Though all mental illness cannot be completely cured, Mental Health day develops an understanding of the value of maintaining good mental health

Let’s focus on the themes for World Mental Health for last four years.

 

World Mental Health day-2021 Mental health in an unequal world

It’s time to change. Let’s end mental health discrimination . If you’re worried about an individual mental health, no matter their gender or caste or religion or any difference, introduce ALAN (Ask Listen Action Network) to them. Which is Ask – when you come in contact with someone, by their look, you may know that something suffers them. If so, ask open ended questions like “ How are you feeling? “ to start a conversation. Show concern but don’t judge. Listen- let them speak! You don’t need to solve problems, they may just need to get stuff off their chest. This may give them a hope to live and feel relieved that some one is there to listen to their story. Action - Help them make a plan of action and set some simple goals. Let them know what help is out there and arrange your next meet up. Say them to make reachable goals and not to expect too much from them. Encourage them and give them hope. Network – Build a network of support together with other friends and family or professionals and relevant organisations. Having good mental health and wellbeing does not mean that you will never experience feelings Or situation that you find difficult. But it does not mean that you feel you have the strength to cope when times are tougher than usual.



World Mental Health day-2020 Mental health for all

Growth of covid pandemic caused increased rate of physical illness and death. It also lead more people to struggle with their mental health. We are facing a crisis of mental health among the world’s youth. Providing young people with early access to good mental healthcare is essential. No one should ever feel embarrassed Or ashamed to talk about it. During this pandemic, self-sigma of mental has decreased in many individuals. Many had suffered from anxiety of the new virus and some for financial stability. Many attempted suicide, due to the depression and anxiety this pandemic and lockdown created. However most of the country spend less budget on mental health. According to the Union Budget 2019-20 of India, Rs.61,398 crore was proposed by the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare , which include Rs.40 crore for mental healthcare. If proper awareness is created, this can be changed. Don’t struggle alone because you’re worried that there are things going on without control and it is big to handle. It is ok to feel not ok. Mental health is the key to a healthy lifestyle. So, mental health for all is the most needed topic to be spoken.

 


 

World Mental Health day-2019 Preventing suicide

To develop global awareness on the very serious but so often neglected issue of suicide every 40 seconds, (WHO)World Health Organization has chose the above topic for 2019. Almost 8,00,000 lives are lost each year. This tragedy of individual ending their life on their own, has also affected their closed friends, relatives and family members. Their family and communities are left behind without the assistance once they had. Though young people represent the future yet suicide is the second leading cause of death among teenagers. Something must be done to ensure that young people have the coping mechanism as they need to face challenges that they encounter in high schools or colleges or universities or in work places. People must ensure that they have access to the support they need. Suicide can affect anyone anywhere no matter how popular you are. Suicide attempts rate are higher in recent days, so particular attention is needed in providing support. Fortunately suicide is preventable, raising awareness is the most needed and this can be done globally by this World Mental Health day. This provides the opportunity to break the stigma, so that many more people able to reach out for help



World Mental Health Day-2018 Mental health for young people

The focus for this year’s is on young people and their mental health. In the changing world, it is vital that younger people get the support they need when they start to become ill. Suicide has become a big issue, big concern for people with younger family members who are suffering from mental health and we need to make sure that we do everything. This day made us look out for what we’re doing to support children, younger people with mental illness and enjoy providing mental health. It’s a great Opportunity to bring a focus on the vital work that we do for youngster. They say one in four people will suffer with a mental health illness ,because it’s so common we all need to talk about it a lot more.

 


Tips to Improve your Mental Health and wellbeing

Connect - connect yourself with outdoors, family and friends, pets or animals, colleagues/Neighbours. Be active -Go for a walk or a ride or any other activity you enjoy Keep learning - learning new skills can give you a sense of achievement and Confidence. Take Notice – be more aware of the present moment, including your feelings and thoughts, your body and the world around you. Enjoy the simple things in life Give – Even the smallest act can count….. a simple, a thank you or larger act such as volunteering can improve your mental health and wellbeing and build new connections!
