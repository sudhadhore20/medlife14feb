VIRUS: A Phenomenon?

We are now facing the wrath of a virus named CORONAVIRUS. Well, everyone is familiar with it. We are living our lives while taking necessary precautions as the virus has an indefinite stay. Every five months are coming up with a new variant and strain. And we are trying to find a proper fitting solution to it.
COVID-19 is fundamentally changing community and social life.

 

What is a virus?
Viruses are microscopic entities that have a core of genetic material, either DNA or RNA. The core is covered with a capsid, a protective coat made of protein. In simple terms, they are made of genetic material inside a protein coating.
According to Wikipedia, "A virus is a submicroscopic infectious agent that replicates only inside the living cells of an organism. Viruses infect all life forms, from animals and plants to microorganisms, including bacteria and Achaea."
A disease does not always appear when a virus is acquired, because the immune system may be able to fight it. However, some viruses can attack certain cells, multiply within them and propagate.
Symptoms of Viral Diseases
fever, fatigue, sore throat Headache. Irritability Sneezing Swollen tonsils Excessive weight loss


TRANSMISSION OF VIRUS


1 Touch: If, for example, a person has the SARS-CoV-2 virus on their hands, and they touch their nose, mouth, or eyes, the virus can enter the body, and they can develop COVID-19.
2.Respiratory secretions: Some viruses can be present in respiratory secretions. A person produces these when they talk, cough, or sneeze. Corona virus is a prime example.
3.Direct contact: Some viruses may spread through direct contact with a person that has the virus. For example, a person can get Hand, Foot, and Mouth Disease (HFMD), after getting into direct contact with affected person.
4.Bodily fluids: viruses like HIV can be spread to others by exchange of blood or semen.
5.Contaminated food or water: Noroviruses are one type of virus that can enter the body when a person consumes contaminated food or water.
6.Insects: Mosquitoes carry a virus that causes Zika from one person to another.
Antibiotics treat bacterial infections, but they cannot treat viral infections. People will need either a vaccination to prevent or antiviral drugs to treat any symptoms. Sometimes, the only option is symptom relief. So it is necessary to take some preventive measures in order to protect ourselves from getting attacked by viruses.

 


 

Preventive measures


1. Wash your hands well. You probably wash your hands after using the bathroom, before preparing or eating food, and after gardening or other dirty tasks. You should wash up after blowing your nose, coughing, sneezing, feeding or stroking your pet, or visiting or caring for a sick person.
2. Cover a cough. Cover your mouth and nose with a tissue when you sneeze or cough. Many respiratory infections are spread by droplets that fall to the ground quickly but can infect others nearby. Others cause airborne transmission in which tiny aerosol particles can travel for longer distances to infect others.
3. Avoid picking your nose or biting your nails, especially if your hands are unwashed.
4. Use face masks. Face masks not only help you avoid getting an infectious respiratory disease but prevent you from infecting others should you be infected.
5. Another way to prevent infection is to live a lifestyle that keeps you healthy. Eat a healthful diet, get routine exercise, and engage in stress reduction.


In the end, the most effective way to be safe from viruses is to take basic preventive measures and take better care of your mental health too. "PREVENTION IS BETTER THAN CURE" literally.
