Bullying and physical abuse in educational institution

Unfortunately bullying is a widespread problem and it can take on everything from your self esteem to your physical health. Even in this developed century, school and college students are still being bullied. Still many picking on people who are smaller than them or different from them or people who don’t speak up for themselves. As said by Jeremy Anderson, a public speaker, “A terrorist gang won’t pick up fight or war with any air force or navy force. They only show their power in shopping mall or with a little coward.” This becomes same with the bullies. They pick fights with or bully only those are smaller than them. Sometimes bullying can be a lot more complicated than that bullies use their power to hurt or control others and they might make an individual feel unsafe in places where the individual should feel protected. School sports teams or clubs have to be safe spaces for everyone

 

You may sometimes don’t feel welcome at school, some girls or boys may laugh at you and make fun you or ignore you altogether, seems like nobody notices you. No one has the right to hurt you or make you feel unsafe. The problem is bullying is had to stop and that’s because most people’s first reaction to bullying is to ignore it. You may feel like, it will go away but the problem with ignoring it is that it won’t stop and it might even get worse maybe you’re embarrassed or you want to believe that it’s not a big deal or maybe you’re afraid that telling will make it worse or that whoever you need to tell won’t understand or be able to help. This isn’t your fault. Don’t be hard on yourself. There are many reasons why bullies do what they do. So this has lot to do with the bully’s personal issues, maybe a little with you. Well known and famous people like Taylor Swift, Christian Bale, Steven Spielberg, Bill Clinton etc. have undergone and experienced this bitter cup of bullying. So, if you’re being bullied, don’t lose hope, you’re in some pretty good company.



What to do if you’re being bullied


These following steps will sure boost up your confidence and help an individual to protect themselves from harm. Bullying has become very common thing like one-third of students are bullied each year. The reason why others get bullied is, the appearance like a nerd, or being unique, or liking unique clothes and so on.
• Don’t try to focus on your weakness. You can also try writing down all the great qualities that make you stand out from the crowd. Good qualities may include your high score in subjects, or your unique talent in music or sports or games. Whatever it is, get it down in a paper. This is the person actually you are. The next time a bully makes you feel like you’re something useless, make this a collection of positive you. Remember the list, whenever you are bullied.
• Build a group of people around. Surround yourself with people who care about you and who appreciate you for exactly who you are. It will be ok if you’re going through this experience with backup. If you’re having trouble finding such a army, try seeking out friends who have similar interests. Look for something like clubs at school or community, or sports whatever you’re interested in.

 


 

• Ignore the bully. This is the most recommended Anti-bullying tactic. Sometimes, all a bully want is to get a reaction from you, if he/she doesn’t receive it from you, they’ll get bored and move on. Another better way to react is, use humour to make them silent. Sometimes by being alone, you become prey to them. So better to be in groups. This makes the bully hard to get near you.
• Get out of there. This may be the other option on line. Find out a way to yourself from the situation. Go find your friends or go get help.
• Find someone you talk to or speak open your problems. Nobody has the right to make you feel unsafe, uncomfortable or unhappy. Find someone who you can trust. It may be your teacher , counsellor, parent, friend and tell them what’s going on. You don’t have to face everything alone. There are people around you, who will accept you and appreciate you as you are. There are people around, who will drop anything to help you. If you’re uncomfortable approaching someone like teachers, there are some places to turn to. Reach for help line. Help line has people who are available to talk anytime, no matter day or night. Don’t let yourself believe that you’re in this bullying issues without any help backups.



Signs of abuse

The very first sign is, you may note is that the children refuses or doesn’t want to go school. They want to spend time alone without socially mingling. Difficulty in sitting down or walking neglected. Abused children show extremes in their behaviour. They may be extremely quiet and calm or quiet volatile. Teachers may note that, they show no interest in studies, resulting in lower of grades. They also seems to suffer from depression and other mental health conditions. They have feelings of insufficiency. There’s probably a sense of unworthiness . This may result an unstable sense of self stemming from emotional, verbal or physical abuse. In an abusive situation, an abuser plants false ideas in your mind. The power of these abuse is also in the words being used. Signs of low self-esteem like pessimism, hostility, lack of motivation, or being a bad communicator are also seen.

 


You may have flashbacks. Flashbacks to previous traumas can come in the form of PTSD. PTSD can affect anyone who were victims of assault or abuse. You may have complex PTSD or CPTSD. CPTSD develop when you suffer repetitive abuse over an extended period of time. The stressful event or situation you were exposed to, was exceptionally threatening which caused you distress. There may be difficulty falling or staying asleep, increased psychological sensitivity, irritability, difficulty regulating your emotions, and difficulty concentrating. You feel numb to your emotions. It can be very difficult to explain how it feels inside. Working out, changing the negative narrative in your head, and practicing mindfulness can help boost your self-esteem. Try to replace negative thought patterns, deals with stress and suicide urges.
