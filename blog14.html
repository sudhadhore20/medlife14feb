OZONE LAYER DEPLETION: AFFECTING HEALTH & ENVIRONMENT

There is an upward spike in the increase of environmental degradation. The biggest challenge that we are facing right now is global warming. Due to the depletion of the ozone layer, UV rays are directly reaching the surface of the earth. That contributes to global warming and has some adverse effects on health too.

 

What is OZONE? How does it affect us?
Ozone is a region of Earth's stratosphere that absorbs most of the Sun's ultraviolet radiation. Without ozone, there will be no protection from the harmful UV radiations that may cause skin cancer and weakened immune systems. Ozone is harmful at ground level.
The ozone layer is present in the lower portion of the earth's atmosphere. It can absorb around 97-99% of the harmful ultraviolet radiation coming from the sun.
OZONE DEPLETION
"Ozone layer depletion is the thinning of the ozone layer present in the upper atmosphere. The main contributor to the exhaustion of the ozone layer is CFCs"
The chlorofluorocarbons react with the ozone layer in the presence of ultraviolet radiation and cause it to break down into oxygen molecules. The result is the depletion of the Ozone Layer. Causes of Ozone Layer Depletion The ozone layer depletion is a big concern and is associated with several factors. The prime causes responsible for the depletion of the ozone layer are listed below:




• Chlorofluorocarbons
Chlorofluorocarbons or CFCs are the main cause of ozone layer depletion. These are released by solvents, spray aerosols, refrigerators, air-conditioners, etc. The molecules of chlorofluorocarbons in the stratosphere are broken down by ultraviolet radiations and release chlorine atoms. These atoms react with ozone and destroy it.
• Compounds of Nitrogen
The compounds of nitrogen such as NO2, NO, N2O are responsible for the depletion of the ozone layer.
• Natural Causes
Some natural processes also cause ozone layer depletion. But it does not cause a lot of damage to the ozone layer. Volcanic eruptions also contribute to the depletion of the ozone layer.
• Global Warming
Global warming also leads to ozone layer depletion. Most of the heat is trapped in the troposphere due to the greenhouse effect. Hence it leads to the lessening of the ozone layer.

 


 

What are the health effects of ozone depletion?
There are many effects of ozone depletion on human health as well as on animals and plants. Like:
1. Cancer: UV rays exposure leads to the development of cataracts, a clouding of the eye’s lens. hence it affects the eyesight of humanity
2. Weakened immune system: Exposure to UV radiations can also result in suppression of immune response to skin cancer, infectious diseases, and other antigens.
3. Food shortage: Strong ultraviolet rays lead to minimal growth, flowering, and photosynthesis in plants. The forests also have to bear the harmful effects of the ultraviolet rays. That will cause a shortage of food supply for humanity.
4. Eyesight: UV rays exposure leads to the development of cataracts, a clouding of the eye’s lens. Hence it affects the eyesight of humanity.
5. Animals: Direct exposure to ultraviolet radiation leads to skin and eye cancer in animals.

How to reduce further depletion of the ozone layer?


There are many ways in which we can save the environment and help the ozone layer to regenerate
• Avoid using ozone-depleting substances such as CFCs. Either we should completely ban them or find a suitable alternative.
• Minimize the use of vehicles to the maximum extent. These release greenhouse gases in large amounts.
• The government should prohibit the use of harmful nitrous oxide that contributes to the depletion of the ozone layer.
• We should try to switch to environmentally friendly products which don't contain bromine or chlorine.
Conclusion
The ozone layer was depleting at an alarming rate before the outbreak of novel CORONAVIRUS. After the pandemic situation, the ozone layer has recovered a bit as people were kept in their houses because of lockdown. Minimal use of vehicles and other factors contributed to the regeneration of the ozone layer. It indicates that if we take measures we can help the environment.
